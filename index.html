About the Qarsherskiyan People

The Qarsherskiyan people are a unique mixed race community whose roots trace back to the Maritime Atlantic Canada and Tidewaters Region of Virginia and the Carolinas during the early 1600s. Their heritage is predominantly of Black and White descent, with many members also having Native American, Romani, and/or Jewish ancestry.

Over generations, Qarsherskiyan families migrated and settled in the Ohio Territory and the northern Appalachian Mountains, spanning from the Roanoke Valley to Newfoundland Island by the early 1800s. This rich tapestry of ancestry has created a vibrant and resilient culture.

Culture & Identity

Qarsherskiyan culture reflects a blend of African, European, Native American, Romani, and Jewish influences. Their traditions include unique forms of music, storytelling, cuisine, and folk art. Community values emphasize kinship, solidarity, and respect for ancestral heritage.

Qarsherskiyans are known for their adaptability and openness, maintaining connections with other multicultural communities while celebrating their distinct identity. The Foundation seeks to preserve and share these traditions for future generations.

Foundation Mission

  • Preserve and promote the history and culture of the Qarsherskiyan people.
  • Support research, education, and community initiatives.
  • Foster dialogue and understanding between Qarsherskiyans and other communities.
  • Encourage pride in mixed heritage and multicultural traditions.
  • Provide resources for families seeking to reconnect with their Qarsherskiyan roots.
